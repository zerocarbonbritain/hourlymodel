HYDROGEN BACKUP
A basic example of using hydrogen to backup a zero carbon electricity supply. Explore hydrogen electrolysis, fuel-cell / gas turbine capacity requirements and hydrogen storage volumes. Note that initial supply demand matching is lower here than more complete models that contain demand side response and other forms of shorter term storage e.g batteries. Actual hydrogen related infrastructure requirements will be lower than this.

DEMAND
An average UK household today uses about 3300 kWh/yr of electricity. Multiplied by 29 million households in the UK in 2030 this would equate to 95.7 TWh/yr of electricity demand. Total electricity demand in ZeroCarbonBritain is about 5x this, in the region of 500 TWh/yr.
